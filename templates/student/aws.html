    Why Should We Learn Aws In 2024

    Cloud Computing services' demand curve continues to flourish and is a critical element in business. According to IDC, from 2018, spending on public cloud services and infrastructure worldwide will rise by 23.8 percent, reaching a total expenditure of $210 billion in 2019. From 2017 to 2024, the projected five-year compound annual growth rate (CAGR) is projected to be 22.5% to reach $370 billion.

    Top 3 Benefits Of Learning AWS

    There is no better time to do so if you want to learn AWS than right now. If you have AWS certification, there are plenty of opportunities for employment or to start your own business. For your consideration, here are three reasons why not only newcomers and cloud professionals will benefit from learning AWS and getting a certification, but also those who want to build a career or improve their business.

    Increased Enterprise Cloud Migration to AWS

    SMBs are rapidly migrating into the cloud to enterprise organizations across industry verticals. Global data center IP traffic will reach 20.6 Zettabytes (ZB) (1.7 ZB per month by the end of 2024, according to Cisco. The growth rate of the global public cloud services market is also expected to reach 17.5 percent

    Cloud Computing expertise is inevitable for IT professionals

    A push towards the cloud is driven by the emergence of cutting-edge technologies like Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

    The Fastest-Growing Public Cloud Service: AWS

    AWS was the first to market its offer of public cloud services and has had a head start over rivals such as Microsoft Azure and Google Cloud Platform for seven years. There were already a hundred and eighty thousand developers on the platform a year after AWS was launched in 2006. The AWS team hasn't looked back since then. AWS expanded its market share to 42 percent in the third quarter of 2017 and reached 49 percent by the first quarter of 2018. AWS is expected to increase its share of the market to 52 percent by 2024.
